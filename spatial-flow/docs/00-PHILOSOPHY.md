## The Problem: Cognitive Discontinuity

For a decade, interfaces have been "flat" -- stacks of static pages that replace each other instantly. When Screen A is destroyed and Screen B appears, the user's brain experiences a **cognitive gap**: it must rebuild its mental model from scratch.

This is the digital equivalent of teleportation without spatial awareness. Imagine if every time you opened a door, you were instantly teleported to the next room with no physical movement. You'd lose your sense of place.

**Traditional web applications commit this crime thousands of times per session.**

---

## The Solution: Spatial Continuity

Spatial Flow treats the interface as a **continuous physical space**. Elements don't teleport -- they travel. Pages don't replace -- they transform. The user's brain maintains its spatial map, reducing cognitive load and creating a sense of **living digital places**.

### The Three Laws of Digital Physics

1. **Conservation of Digital Mass**
   An element never vanishes. It moves, shrinks, morphs, or hides behind something. `opacity: 0 -> 1` without spatial origin is forbidden.

2. **Continuity of Identity**
   If a "Search Bar" exists in View A and View B, it is the SAME object. It must physically travel between positions. This is not decoration -- it is the fundamental principle of object permanence.

3. **Orthogonal Order**
   Movement follows strict grid lines. Diagonal movement is forbidden. The brain processes orthogonal movement 3x faster than arbitrary curves. Chaos is never acceptable.

## Visiomorphism: The New Design Paradigm

**Visiomorphism** (Visual Isomorphism) is the successor to Flat Design and Skeuomorphism.

| Paradigm | Era | Principle |
|:---------|:----|:----------|
| Skeuomorphism | 2007-2013 | Digital mimics analog appearance |
| Flat Design | 2013-2023 | Minimal, static, efficient |
| **Visiomorphism** | 2023+ | **Form follows function mutation** |

In Visiomorphism:
- A button **becomes** a modal
- A list item **expands** into a detail view
- A thumbnail **grows** into a fullscreen image
- A card's content **dissolves** and **re-emerges** as a new form

The object doesn't disappear. It *becomes*.

## The Follow Flow Principle

Traditional mobile apps use "Push" navigation: content moves **opposite** to the gesture direction (swipe right = content goes left). This is unintuitive.

Spatial Flow uses **Follow Flow**: content moves in the **same direction** as the user's attention.

- Tap a tab to the right -> content moves right
- Tap "Back" (left arrow) -> content moves left

This leverages the brain's innate spatial navigation (hippocampus) rather than fighting it.

## Parallel Worlds Architecture

We reject the idea that "Mobile" is a squashed "Desktop".

- **Two Worlds**: Mobile and Desktop are distinct spatial topologies with their own rules.
- **One Soul**: They share the same application state and identity.
- **No Forced Unification**: Mobile is touch-first (swipes, rotation). Desktop is mouse-first (hovers, precision clicks).
- **Device-Specific Protocols**: Gyroscopic Landscape Shift [GLS] is mobile-only. Desktop would never rotate.
