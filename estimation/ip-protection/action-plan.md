## Licensing Strategy

### Recommended: Dual License

**Open Source (Community)**:
- License: MIT or Apache 2.0
- Covers: Core animation engine, Soul Constants base
- Why: Maximum adoption, community trust

**Commercial (Premium)**:
- License: Proprietary / BSL (Business Source License)
- Covers: Figma Sync Protocol, premium components, enterprise features
- Why: Protects revenue-generating IP
- Model: Similar to MariaDB BSL or Sentry's approach

### Alternative: AGPL + Commercial Exception
- AGPL for open source (forces competitors to also open source)
- Commercial license for companies that don't want AGPL obligations
- More aggressive but effective against corporate copycats

## AI-Specific Risks

### Risk: Training Data Leakage
- Conversations with AI assistants may be used for model training
- **Mitigation**: 
  - Check each AI provider's data retention policy
  - Use "opt-out" features where available
  - Don't share the most sensitive architectural details unnecessarily

### Risk: Someone Prompts a Similar Framework
- An AI could generate something conceptually similar if prompted well
- **Mitigation**:
  - Speed to market (publish first)
  - Build community (people follow creators, not code)
  - The implementation details and polish are what matter

### Risk: AI-Generated Prior Art Claims
- Someone could claim they independently created similar concepts
- **Mitigation**:
  - Timestamped public publications
  - Git history with gradual development (not a single commit)
  - Article publication dates on third-party platforms
